Muhammad Ali

The man who inspired millions to take up boxing

Here's a time line of Muhammad Ali's life:

  • 1942 - Muhammad Ali (born Cassius Marcellus Clay Jr.) was born on January 17th in Louisville, Kentucky, United States.
  • 1954 - Ali began boxing at the age of 12 when he was introduced to the sport after having his bike stolen.
  • 1960 - Ali won a gold medal in the light heavyweight division at the Summer Olympics in Rome, Italy.
  • 1964 - On February 25th, Ali defeated Sonny Liston to become the heavyweight boxing champion of the world at the age of 22. He also announced his conversion to Islam and changed his name from Cassius Clay to Muhammad Ali.
  • 1967 - Ali refused to be drafted into the U.S. military due to his religious beliefs and opposition to the Vietnam War. As a result, he was stripped of his boxing titles, banned from the sport, and faced legal battles.
  • 1970 - Ali's conviction for draft evasion was overturned by the Supreme Court of the United States.
  • 1971 - Ali's boxing license was reinstated, and he competed in his first professional fight after a three-and-a-half-year hiatus, defeating Jerry Quarry.
  • 1974 - On October 30th, Ali faced George Foreman in "The Rumble in the Jungle" in Kinshasa, Zaire (now the Democratic Republic of Congo), where he used his "rope-a-dope" strategy to win the fight and regain the heavyweight title.
  • 1975 - Ali had his famous "Thrilla in Manila" bout against Joe Frazier in the Philippines, winning by technical knockout in the 14th round after a grueling match.
  • 1981 - Ali retired from professional boxing after facing several more fights and retiring with a record of 56 wins and 5 losses.
  • 1996 - Ali lit the Olympic cauldron at the Summer Olympics in Atlanta, Georgia, USA, eliciting a powerful and emotional response from the crowd.
  • 2016 - Muhammad Ali passed away on June 3rd in Scottsdale, Arizona, after a long battle with Parkinson's disease.
